🎯 Key Regulatory Principles This Activity Covered
1
Non-device software (general wellness, scheduling, admin tools) is not regulated by FDA — but "not regulated" does not mean "safe." Institutional governance still applies.
2
CDSS is exempt only when the clinician can independently review the basis for the recommendation without relying on the software to do so. Display of data ≠ clinical recommendation.
3
The critical SaMD trigger: does the software analyze medical images, process physiological signals, or drive clinical management of a serious condition? If yes — FDA oversight applies.
4
"FDA cleared" via 510(k) means substantial equivalence to a predicate device — not demonstrated clinical superiority, safety across all populations, or equity performance.
5
Exempt tools still carry liability. If a CDSS-exempt tool contributes to patient harm, the institution and clinician may bear liability even without FDA oversight involvement.
