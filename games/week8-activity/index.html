Module 1 - Implementation Decision

You are the Director of Nursing Practice & Innovation at Memorial Community, a 240-bed safety-net hospital. Vendor "AcuteIQ" has proposed a sepsis CDSS for go-live in 90 days.

The Tool - AcuteIQ Sepsis Predictor v3.2

EHR-integrated predictive model. Generates risk scores hourly for adult inpatients. Vendor reports AUC 0.87, sensitivity 82%, specificity 79%.

Validation: Internal 80/20 split (single academic medical center, n=52,000) Population: 62% White, 18% Black, 9% Hispanic, 6% Asian, 5% other Calibration: Not reported Equity stratification: Not reported Funding: Vendor-sponsored; lead author employed by AcuteIQ Your population: 65% Black/Hispanic, 38% Medicaid, 12% Spanish-preferred, sepsis base rate ~9%

Module 3 - Governance Simulation

You chair the Memorial AI Governance Committee. The vote on AcuteIQ adoption is today. You hear three positions, then call the question.

Committee Voices

CMIO - Adopt now
"AUC 0.87 is best-in-class. Pilot data is solid. We can address gaps post-deployment."
Frontline RN - Hesitant
"Override rate 42% for Spanish-preferred patients is a workflow problem AND a justice problem. We need fixes BEFORE go-live."
Equity Officer - Decline
"11% sensitivity gap by language preference. We can't deploy a tool that under-detects sepsis in 12% of our patients."
Patient/Family Advisor - Curious
"Patients haven't been told this tool would shape their care. What's our consent and disclosure plan?"

The Six Stewardship Stances You Practiced

  1. Demand stratified evidence at the patient level the model will reach.
  2. Translate aggregate metrics into local PPV at your institution's base rate.
  3. Treat override-rate disparity as clinical telemetry - not a user-training issue.
  4. Pre-define stop/go criteria and act on them when they trip.
  5. Make transparency, consent, and human review patient-facing artifacts, not policies-on-paper.
  6. Use Adopt-with-conditions as a leadership tool - not as an excuse to delay action.
